Discover the city of Syracuse

History of Syracuse

George Henry Payne originaly started up the first salt works in Syracuse. He was able to harvest salt crystals by evaporating the salt water in special ponds. In August of 1885, William W. Galbraith bought George Payne's salt works. He adopted the brand name Syracuse, named after Syracuse, New York, where salt was being produced at the time. The name "Syracuse" was subsequently adopted as the name of our city.

Population

The current population of Syracuse is 33,703. It is the 30th largest city in Utah and the 1350th largest city in the United States. Syracuse spans over 10 miles, and has a population density of 3,317 people per square mile. Syracuse is currently growing at a rate of 2.37% annually and its population has increased by 4.86% since the most recent census.

Location of Syracuse

Syracuse is a city that is located in Davis County, Utah. It is situated between the Great Salt Lake and interstate 15, which is about 30 miles north of Salt lake City, and west of both Clearfield and Layton.

Businesses in Syracuse

The economy of Syracuse employs 21,629 people and has an unemployment rate of 2.9%. Some of the largest industries in Syracuse are Professional Services numbering 174 in 2021. There are 58 Personal Services & Consultants, and 54 Builders & Contractors. With such a fast growing population, investors can take advantage of this fast developing area.

Economic Development

As our city grows, residents will need increased amount of goods, serives, and places to work. The city of Syracuse welcomes growth and we would assist and support those who wish to invest in us.
